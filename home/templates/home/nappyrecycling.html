Nappy Recycling Process

Recycling nappies is a simple yet effective way to reduce waste and contribute to a cleaner environment. Our process includes collection, sorting, and recycling of used nappies into reusable materials.

Is Burning Diapers the Best Solution for Kenya? Certainly Not In Kenya, millions of disposable diapers end up in residual waste bins every day, destined for incineration.
While this is the common method, it is far from ideal. When it comes to disposable diapers, incineration is not sustainable. Although burning generates energy, it also means losing all the materials forever. Diapers are complex products made from various plastics. Burning these plastics is problematic for two main reasons. Firstly, it destroys a valuable raw material that could be recycled. Secondly, incinerating plastics is detrimental to both the climate and the environment. a cutting-edge and sustainable recycling plant specifically designed for diapers.

Step 1: Collection

Used nappies are collected from households and commercial establishments.

Step 2: Sorting and Cleaning

The collected nappies are sorted and cleaned to remove any contaminants.

Step 3: Recycling

The cleaned nappies are processed to extract recyclable materials, which are then used to manufacture new products.

Benefits of Nappy Recycling

Recycling nappies not only helps in reducing the amount of waste that ends up in landfills but also conserves resources and reduces pollution. Here are some key benefits:

  • Reduces landfill waste
  • Conserves natural resources
  • Reduces greenhouse gas emissions
  • Promotes a cleaner and healthier environment
